## Concept 1 — The Eye Mark

**Icon:** Abstract angular eye/lens shape with a lime rectangular "screen" as the iris — simultaneously referencing vision, surveillance/analytics, and DOOH display screens.

**Strongest use case:** Platform sidebar (dark background) and pitch deck headers. The eye metaphor communicates *attention* and *viewership* which is the core value proposition of a DOOH ad network — eyeballs on screens.

**Refinement suggestions:** Push the iris rectangle to be more explicitly screen-shaped (16:9 ratio). Consider adding a subtle angular eyelid line to sharpen the lens metaphor. The wordmark tracking should be tightened to -0.05em for that Inter Tight premium feel.

## Concept 2 — The Screen Signal

**Icon:** Stylised rectangular display screen with a broadcast signal arc above it — directly represents DOOH screens in gym venues, with the signal element adding the "network/media" layer.

**Strongest use case:** Rate cards, media proposals, and sales collateral where the product needs to be immediately understood. This concept has the clearest literal connection to what GymGaze *does* — putting ads on gym screens. Zero ambiguity.

**Refinement suggestions:** The signal arc could be reduced to 2 clean lines (not a full wifi fan) for a more premium, less "telecom" feel. Screen proportions should favour 16:9 over square. Consider a hairline lime border on the screen with a dark interior fill to suggest the screen as a canvas.

## Concept 3 — The GG Monogram

**Icon:** Double-G lettermark — one lime, one white — inside a dark rounded-square container. Bold, app-icon-ready, and immediately readable at small sizes. The contrast between lime and white G creates visual tension that reads as "dual" — gym + gaze, venue + advertiser.

**Strongest use case:** App icon, favicon, and any context where a compact square mark is needed. This is the most scalable concept and the strongest for digital product contexts. Business cards would also hit hard with this mark embossed.

**Refinement suggestions:** The two G letterforms need careful kerning so they don't read as "66". Consider making one G a filled/solid form and the other an outline form rather than just colour-differentiated. A subtle negative-space connection between the two G shapes would elevate this significantly.

## Concept 4 — The Track Mark

**Icon:** Three parallel horizontal lines of decreasing length — the longest in lime, shorter ones fading. Simultaneously reads as: running track lanes (gym/fitness), a signal strength indicator (tech/broadcast), and a data bar chart (media analytics). Three meanings in one mark — that's good design.

**Strongest use case:** Dashboard UI header, dark-mode sidebar, and anywhere the brand needs to feel data-forward and dynamic. Weakest on white backgrounds without strong contrast adjustment. Best as a secondary/supporting mark alongside a primary wordmark.

**Refinement suggestions:** Left-align the lines (not centred) to create a stronger sense of motion/direction. Consider making the second line #D4FF4F at 60% opacity and the third at 30% for a more intentional fade. Could also work as a loading/progress animation on the platform.

## Recommendation

**Primary recommendation: Concept 1 — The Eye Mark**, with Concept 3 as the companion app icon.

Here's the logic: The Eye Mark is the most *ownable* concept. "Gaze" is literally in the brand name — an eye mark is the natural expression of that word, and it avoids being too literal about the DOOH/screen side (which could date the brand as technology evolves). It's premium, abstract enough to be versatile, and sharp enough to hold the athletic-tech energy.

The Screen Signal (C2) is strongest for sales contexts but risks looking like any other AdTech or streaming logo. The Track Mark (C4) is the weakest standalone mark but could live beautifully as a UI motif inside the platform itself.

**Proposed brand system:**
- **Primary logo:** Concept 1 (Eye Mark) — all external brand contexts
- **App icon:** Concept 3 (GG Monogram) — favicons, mobile, platform UI
- **Decorative motif:** Concept 4 lines — used as a UI texture/pattern, not a primary logo
